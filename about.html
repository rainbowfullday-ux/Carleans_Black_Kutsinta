History

Carleans began in 2015 when husband and wife Rolan Leonardo Paez and Carla Aran Paez started sharing their homemade kakanin online. What began as simple posts eventually grew attention, especially when they introduced their black kutsinta. The idea came from their habit of experimenting with flavors and presentation. Since the color black is usually seen as unappetizing in food, they wanted to challenge that and give it a new image by showing that it can look good and taste just as good as the traditional version. They kept the same classic kutsinta flavor people love, then made it more unique with creamy caramel sauce and toasted coconut. As more people discovered their product, the small home-based business slowly expanded. Carleans opened their first branch in Calumpang, followed by Pantok and Angono. Along the way, they added more Filipino favorites like puto cake, leche flan, and maja blanca, which helped the brand grow while keeping its roots in familiar homemade kakanin.

Mission

To provide customers with high-quality Filipino delicacies every day, ensuring that each product maintains consistent taste and quality.

Vision

To be the household name for rice cakes in the Philippines. When people think of "kakanin," Carlean's should be the first name that comes to mind, recognized as the country's best delicacy.
